# **🎯** 

# **Website Design Best Practices (Kids Music School Conversion Funnel)**

  

_Final Version – Copy/Paste into Obsidian_

---

## **1. Core Objectives**

- Convert parents into enquiries/enrolments.
    
- Capture warm leads through a **“Lesson Info Pack”** download funnel.
    
- Build trust within seconds of landing.
    
- Reduce uncertainty and friction at every step.
    
- Make all pages scannable, friendly, and parent-focused.
    
- Use real photos of teachers, kids, classrooms, and lessons.
    
- Provide early **pricing clarity** to reduce hesitation.
    

---

## **2. Parent Psychology (What Drives Decisions)**

  

Parents care about:

- **Safety** (background checks, safeguarding, small groups)
    
- **Trust** (qualified teachers, real photos, structured curriculum)
    
- **Clarity** (age groups, timetable, pricing, expectations)
    
- **Convenience** (easy enrolment, easy contact, mobile-friendly everything)
    
- **Progress** (defined outcomes, exams, skills, confidence)
    
- **Low-commitment information** (“Info Pack” gives reassurance before enrolling)
    
- **Transparency** (pricing upfront = huge trust boost)
    

  

Everything must signal:

**“Your child will be safe, happy, and progressing.”**

---

## **3. High-Converting Page Structure**

  

Each page should follow this order:

1. **Clear header** (what this page is)
    
2. **Key value/benefit** (why it matters to parents)
    
3. **Main content** (lessons, teachers, timetable, pricing, etc.)
    
4. **Trust blocks** (testimonials, teacher credentials, safety info)
    
5. **Pricing teaser where relevant** (“Lessons from €X/week”)
    
6. **Info Pack CTA (secondary, low-commitment)**
    
7. **Primary CTA** (“Enrol Now”)
    

  

Short sections.

Direct headers.

No clutter.

---

## **4. Visual Rules**

- Use real photos of children in lessons (NO stock).
    
- Prioritise warm, friendly colours; soft shapes; approachable visuals.
    
- Kid-friendly but **not childish** — parents are the target.
    
- Avoid intense colours or animations that distract from CTAs.
    
- Show:
    
    - Smiling kids
        
    - Small groups
        
    - Teachers interacting
        
    - Instruments in use
        
    - Rooms that feel welcoming
        
    

---

## **5. Copywriting Rules**

  

Copy must be:

- Clear
    
- Reassuring
    
- Benefit-driven
    
- Short and direct
    
- Parent-focused
    
- Friendly in tone
    
- Free of jargon
    

  

Focus on outcomes:

- Confidence
    
- Creativity
    
- Progress
    
- Joy
    
- Safety
    
- Structure
    

  

Add microcopy for reassurance:

- “Takes less than 3 minutes”
    
- “No obligation — just info”
    
- “We’ll help you choose the right class”
    

  

Use the formula:

**Feature → Benefit → Parent Outcome**

Example:

“Weekly updates → Children stay motivated → You see real improvement.”

---

## **6. CTA Best Practices**

  

### **Primary CTA (High intent)**

  

**“Enrol Now”**

  

### **Secondary CTA (Low intent but high volume)**

  

**“Download Lesson Info Pack”**

  

Rules:

- These two CTAs must appear throughout the site.
    
- Secondary CTA captures warm leads who aren’t ready to enrol yet.
    
- Place CTAs:
    
    - Hero section
        
    - Mid-page
        
    - Bottom of page
        
    
- Keep CTAs consistent across pages.
    

  

Avoid multiple competing buttons.

---

## **7. Navigation Best Practices**

  

Navigation must be simple and parent-friendly.

  

Include:

- Home
    
- Lessons
    
- Teachers
    
- Timetable
    
- Pricing
    
- Enrolment
    
- Contact
    

  

Avoid creative labels.

Clarity always wins.

---

## **8. Forms (Critical Conversion Elements)**

  

### **Two forms are required:**

  

#### **1. Enrolment Form (High Intent)**

- One single form using conditional logic
    
- Minimal fields
    
- Mobile-first
    
- Reassurance text (“We’ll match your child with the right teacher”)
    

  

#### **2. Info Pack Download Form (Low Intent, High Volume)**

- Very short:
    
    - Name
        
    - Email
        
    - Phone
        
    - Optional child age
        
    
- After submission:
    
    - Parent gets PDF
        
    - You + school get the lead
        
    
- Acts as “soft enquiry”
    
- Extremely high conversion rate
    

  

Parents abandon forms when:

- Too long
    
- Confusing
    
- Unclear next steps
    
- No reassurance
    

  

Include a micro-line:

“After you submit, we’ll contact you with available times.”

---

## **9. Social Proof Requirements**

  

Testimonials must be:

- Specific
    
- Parent-focused
    
- Mention progress or confidence
    
- Mention teacher by name
    
- Include parent name + child’s age
    

  

Example:

“Emma (8) has grown so much in confidence since joining Sarah’s piano class.”

  

No generic or anonymous reviews.

---

## **10. Trust Signals**

  

Recommended:

- Teacher bios
    
- Garda vetting
    
- Qualifications (ABRSM, RIAM, Yamaha)
    
- Real photos of lessons
    
- Term structure
    
- Clear pricing
    
- Info Pack download
    
- Testimonials
    

  

Optional but strong:

- Exam results
    
- Recital photos
    
- Community involvement
    

  

Avoid:

- Fake awards
    
- Random badges
    
- Overly flashy seals
    

---

## **11. Mobile-First Design**

  

Since 80–90% of parents browse on mobile:

- Use large buttons
    
- Clear font sizes
    
- Keep text short
    
- Avoid heavy, tall hero images
    
- Make timetable scroll-friendly
    
- Make forms extremely simple
    
- Keep CTA visible
    
- Ensure pricing is clear on mobile
    

---

## **12. Performance Best Practices**

- Compress all images
    
- Avoid heavy animation libraries
    
- Keep Lighthouse scores 90+
    
- Limit font families
    
- Optimise hero image
    
- Avoid large JS bundles
    
- Don’t overuse GSAP
    

---

## **13. SEO for Local Music Schools**

  

Focus on:

- Location keywords (“Music Lessons Kilkenny”)
    
- Instruments (“piano lessons for kids”)
    
- Age groups
    
- Term dates
    
- Pricing clarity
    
- Testimonials
    

  

Critical SEO pages:

- Home
    
- Lessons
    
- Pricing
    
- Timetable
    
- Contact
    

  

### **SEO Benefit: Info Pack PDF**

  

Google indexes PDF content, so including:

- Location
    
- Instruments
    
- Age groups
    
- Pricing
    
- Term dates
    
    …boosts SEO indirectly.
    

---

## **14. Funnel Psychology**

  

Your funnel has **two conversion paths**:

  

### **Primary Conversion (High Intent)**

  

→ _Enrolment Form_

  

### **Secondary Conversion (Low Intent, High Volume)**

  

→ _Lesson Info Pack Download_

  

This dual-path funnel:

- Captures parents who are ready
    
- Captures parents who are unsure
    
- Creates more follow-up opportunities
    
- Increases total leads by 2–5×
    

  

Each page should push parents toward one of these two actions.

---

## **15. Pages That Convert Best**

  

Order of impact:

1. Home
    
2. Pricing
    
3. Timetable
    
4. Lessons
    
5. Teachers
    
6. Enrolment
    
7. Contact
    

  

Design with intent:

- Home = trust + clarity + pricing + info pack
    
- Pricing = transparency + info pack fallback
    
- Timetable = urgency + enrol CTA
    
- Lessons = structured + info pack CTA
    
- Teachers = trust
    
- Enrolment = friction-free
    
- Contact = simplicity
    

---

## **16. Avoid These Mistakes**

- No pricing on Home
    
- No Info Pack download
    
- Overly long text
    
- Generic stock photos
    
- Confusing nav
    
- Too many CTAs
    
- Hidden pricing
    
- Cluttered timetable
    
- Teacher bios that are generic or cold
    
- Overuse of animations
    
- Only relying on the contact form (loses 70% of leads)
