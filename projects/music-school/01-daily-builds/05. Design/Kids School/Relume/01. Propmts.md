# **✅ 1. How Relume Prompting Works (Best Practice)**

  

Relume’s AI builder uses **three input layers**:

---

## **LEVEL 1 — SITE MAP LEVEL**

  

This is the **global briefing**.

  

What you put here determines:

- Which pages your whole site will contain
    
- Tone and style of the site
    
- The type of business
    
- Overall goals
    
- Important constraints
    
- Must-have features
    

  

Think of this like your **creative brief**.

  

👉 If you get this wrong, ALL generated pages will be wrong.

  

**Best practice:**

- Describe the company simply
    
- Describe the target audience
    
- State the primary goal
    
- List secondary goals
    
- List extra requirements (blog, teachers page, enrolment forms, etc.)
    

---

## **LEVEL 2 — PAGE LEVEL**

  

This is the **per-page goal description**.

  

It tells Relume:

- What this page must do
    
- Who it’s for
    
- The information hierarchy
    
- What the CTA should be
    

  

**Best practice:**

Be extremely specific with what the page must accomplish.

  

Example patterns:

- “This page must highlight…”
    
- “This page should convince visitors that…”
    
- “This page must include…”
    
- “This page must avoid…”
    
- “The CTA should focus on…”
    

---

## **LEVEL 3 — SECTION LEVEL**

  

This is the **micro-level prompt**.

  

Used only when you want control over a specific section.

  

You define:

- **Section name** (Hero, Features, Testimonials, FAQ, etc.)
    
- **Section description** (what info goes here)
    

  

Best practice:

- Keep descriptions short
    
- Do NOT describe visuals (“big image, two columns”) — Relume handles that
    
- Focus on **what content goes here**, not styling

# **LEVEL 1 — SITE MAP LEVEL (GLOBAL BRIEF)**

  

### **Company Description**

  

A modern, high-converting website template built specifically for children’s music schools. Designed to help parents quickly understand programs, pricing, term dates, class schedules, and teacher qualifications. The template uses clear structure, trust-building content, and parent-friendly copy to increase enquiries. It is optimised for local SEO and built to convert visitors into enrolments with minimal friction.

  

### **Primary Goal**

  

Convert parents of school-age children into enquiries and bookings.

  

### **Secondary Goals**

- Clearly communicate what classes are available for each age group
    
- Build trust through teacher bios, qualifications, and photos
    
- Explain term dates, pricing, and expectations clearly
    
- Help parents understand the benefits of structured music education
    
- Increase clarity, reduce confusion, and lower decision friction
    
- Create a seamless funnel from landing → lessons → pricing → enrolment
    

  

### **Other Relevant Notes**

- Include a Courses / Lessons page (focused on age groups + instruments)
    
- Include a Teachers page (trust building)
    
- Include a Timetable page (easy scheduling for parents)
    
- Include a Pricing / Fees page (term structure, deposits, discounts)
    
- Include ONE Enrolment Form (with child/adult branching)
    
- Include FAQ (attendance, payments, ages, practice, exams)
    
- Include Contact page
    
- Include optional Gallery page (strong emotional hook for parents)
    
- Keep every page 4–8 sections long
    
- Avoid clutter; prioritise clarity
    
- Emphasise safety, trustworthiness, and qualified teachers
    
- Design must feel warm, approachable, and parent-friendly

# **⭐** 

# **LEVEL 2 — PAGE PROMPTS (PARENT-FOCUSED, MAX CONVERSION)**

  

Below is the **page-level Relume format** rewritten specifically for kids/parents.

---

## **Landing Page Prompt**

  

“Primary goal: convert parents into enquiries.

  

Highlight: the school’s credibility, qualified teachers, age-based programs, structured learning, safety, and child development benefits.

  

Include: a strong hero section with value proposition, featured lessons by age group, benefits of music for kids, teacher spotlight, parent testimonials, and a clear CTA (‘Enrol Your Child’).

  

Keep copy short, visual, and trust-driven.”

---

## **Courses / Lessons Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: clearly present all children’s programs by instrument and age group.

  

Each course should list:

- suitable ages
    
- skill level
    
- lesson length
    
- benefits
    
- what children learn
    
- expectations for parents
    

  

Must help parents choose the right program quickly.”

---

## **Timetable Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: show weekly class schedule for children’s group lessons.

  

Include: days, times, age groups, lesson types, and availability.

  

Must be easy to scan and help parents plan around school hours.”

---

## **Pricing & Fees Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: clearly communicate term fees, lesson lengths, deposit rules, payment dates, sibling discounts, and group vs private prices.

  

Keep layout simple with no hidden details. Transparency builds trust for parents.”

---

## **About Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: establish total trust.

  

Include: school mission, child-focused teaching philosophy, safety, qualifications, experience, exam preparation, and any recognitions (e.g., Yamaha licensing).

  

Parents must feel the school is safe, structured, and highly professional.”

---

## **Teachers Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: humanise the school and build credibility.

  

Include teacher photos, qualifications, years teaching, instruments taught, and a warm, friendly description of their teaching style.

  

Parents choose teachers based on trust.”

---

## **FAQ Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: reduce parent anxiety and remove objections.

  

Include: attendance rules, missed lessons, payment policies, term dates, required books/instruments, what to expect in the first lesson, practice guidelines, exams, and safety.”

---

## **Enrolment Form Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: funnel parents directly into a single, simple form.

  

Include: child name, age, instrument, preferred days, parent contact details, beginner/intermediate option, and notes.

  

One form for all ages.”

---

## **Contact Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: make it easy for parents to get answers.

  

Include phone, email, address, embedded map, and CTA.

  

Keep layout clean and simple.”
