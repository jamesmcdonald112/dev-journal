# **✅ 1. How Relume Prompting Works (Best Practice)**

  

Relume’s AI builder uses **three input layers**:

---

## **LEVEL 1 — SITE MAP LEVEL**

  

This is the **global briefing**.

  

What you put here determines:

- Which pages your whole site will contain
    
- Tone and style of the site
    
- The type of business
    
- Overall goals
    
- Important constraints
    
- Must-have features
    

  

Think of this like your **creative brief**.

  

👉 If you get this wrong, ALL generated pages will be wrong.

  

**Best practice:**

- Describe the company simply
    
- Describe the target audience
    
- State the primary goal
    
- List secondary goals
    
- List extra requirements (blog, teachers page, enrolment forms, etc.)
    

---

## **LEVEL 2 — PAGE LEVEL**

  

This is the **per-page goal description**.

  

It tells Relume:

- What this page must do
    
- Who it’s for
    
- The information hierarchy
    
- What the CTA should be
    

  

**Best practice:**

Be extremely specific with what the page must accomplish.

  

Example patterns:

- “This page must highlight…”
    
- “This page should convince visitors that…”
    
- “This page must include…”
    
- “This page must avoid…”
    
- “The CTA should focus on…”
    

---

## **LEVEL 3 — SECTION LEVEL**

  

This is the **micro-level prompt**.

  

Used only when you want control over a specific section.

  

You define:

- **Section name** (Hero, Features, Testimonials, FAQ, etc.)
    
- **Section description** (what info goes here)
    

  

Best practice:

- Keep descriptions short
    
- Do NOT describe visuals (“big image, two columns”) — Relume handles that
    
- Focus on **what content goes here**, not styling

# **⭐ LEVEL 2 — PAGE LEVEL PROMPTS (WHAT YOU PASTE PER PAGE)**

  

Below are **templates** tailored for a music school.

## **Landing Page Prompt**

  

**Paste:**

  

“Primary goal: convert parents and adult learners into enquiries.

Highlight key benefits: experienced teachers, structured courses, term dates, pricing transparency, easy enrolment.

Include: hero section with clear value proposition, featured programs, term dates, teacher highlight, testimonials, clear CTA (“Enrol Now”).

Avoid long text. Focus on clarity and trust.”

---

## **Courses & Lessons Page Prompt**

  

“Goals: clearly list all instruments and classes offered (piano, guitar, drums, flute, violin, singing, group classes, Yamaha programs).

Each course needs: age range, skill level, duration, benefits, what students learn, who it’s suitable for.

Should help visitors pick the right program.”

---

## **Timetable Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: display clear weekly schedule for group classes.

Include: days, times, age group, class type.

Make it easy to understand availability and terms.

Avoid clutter.”

---

## **Pricing & Fees Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: clearly explain term pricing, private vs group fees, deposit requirements, payment rules, and discounts.

Prioritise clarity and transparency.”

---

## **About Page Prompt**

  

“Goals: tell the academy’s story, highlight the mission, educational philosophy, qualifications, and achievements (e.g., Yamaha licensing).

Include teacher highlight intro.

Build trust and credibility.”

---

## **Teachers Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: introduce each teacher with bio, qualifications, specialisations, teaching philosophy, and a friendly photo.

Make teachers feel trustworthy and skilled.”

---

## **FAQ Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: answer common questions about attendance, payment, class structure, practice expectations, exams, ages, etc.”

---

## **Forms Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: central location listing all enrolment forms (Kids, Adults, Yamaha, Re-enrolment).

Each item links externally to Google Forms or internal forms.”

---

## **Contact Page Prompt**

  

“Goal: provide contact info, phone, email, address, map, and CTA to enquire.

Simple and clear.”
