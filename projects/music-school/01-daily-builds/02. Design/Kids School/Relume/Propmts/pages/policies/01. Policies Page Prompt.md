
**Goal:**

Present policies in a way that feels _supportive_, _fair_, and _parent-friendly_ — not strict or intimidating.

Everything must be easy to scan, gentle in tone, and designed to reassure parents.

  

Tone: warm, transparent, supportive, safety-focused.

---

## **Page Structure**

  

### **Navbar**

---

### **Header Section**

  

Warm, clear headline:

**“Our School Policies — Simple, Fair & Parent-Friendly.”**

  

Short intro:

Explain that policies exist to keep children safe, ensure smooth scheduling, and help parents know exactly what to expect.

Reassurance line:

**“All policies are easy to understand and designed with families in mind.”**

---

### **Attendance Policy Section**

  

Scannable bullets outlining:

- attendance expectations
    
- how to report an absence
    
- what to do for late arrivals
    
- make-up class rules (kept simple and realistic)
    
- friendly communication guidelines
    

  

Tone must feel helpful, not strict.

---

### **Payment Policy Section**

  

Clear, transparent explanation of:

- term payment schedule
    
- accepted payment methods
    
- deposits (only if applicable)
    
- due dates
    
- how late payments are handled (gentle tone)
    
- reassurance: **“No hidden fees — everything is explained upfront.”**
    

---

### **Safeguarding & Child Protection Section**

  

Warm, trust-building reassurance covering:

- Garda vetting / background checks
    
- qualified, trained staff
    
- supervised, safe learning environments
    
- safeguarding policies and standards
    
- how parents can raise concerns
    

  

Tone: confident, calm, parent-centric.

---

### **Refunds & Cancellations Section**

  

Simple and fair wording explaining:

- refund rules
    
- cancellation timelines
    
- mid-term withdrawal policy
    
- how credits or rescheduling work
    

  

Must be extremely easy for parents to understand.

---

### **Parent Conduct & Communication Section**

###  **(optional but recommended)**

  

Gentle, supportive guidelines on:

- parent–teacher communication
    
- arrival and pickup expectations
    
- waiting area etiquette
    
- keeping a positive, supportive environment for all children
    

  

Tone: positive, community-focused.

---

### **FAQ Section**

  

Short, clear answers to common parent questions:

- “What if my child needs to change days?”
    
- “What if we miss multiple classes?”
    
- “What if my child wants to switch instruments?”
    
- “How do I contact the school about a concern?”
    

  

Each answer must be brief and reassuring.

---

### **CTA Section**

  

Secondary CTA:

  

**“Prefer a full overview? Download the Lesson Info Pack.”**

Button links to the Info Pack form/section.
