
**Goal:**

Make it effortless for parents to ask questions or request guidance. Remove friction, build reassurance, and set expectations around fast, friendly communication.

  

**Tone:**

Warm, professional, supportive, parent-focused.

---

## **Page Structure**

  

### **Navbar**

---

### **Header Section**

  

Warm, friendly headline:

**“We’re Here to Help — Get in Touch Anytime.”**

  

Short intro explaining that parents can contact the school with questions about:

- lessons
    
- availability
    
- pricing
    
- next steps
    

  

Emphasise friendliness, support, and fast replies.

  

Include a real, warm classroom or teacher photo.

---

### **Contact Form Section (Primary Interaction)**

  

Main contact form with the following fields:

- Name
    
- Email
    
- Phone
    
- Child’s Age
    
- Message
    

  

Reassurance microcopy:

**“We reply quickly and are happy to guide you through lessons, availability, and next steps.”**

  

Form must be:

- simple
    
- mobile-first
    
- parent-focused
    
- minimal friction
    

---

### **Contact Details Section**

  

Clear display of:

- phone number
    
- email address
    
- school address
    
- opening hours
    

  

Reassurance line:

**“No question is too small — we’re here to help.”**

---

### **Location & Access Section**

  

Embedded map showing the school’s location.

  

Add concise info on:

- parking
    
- accessibility
    
- public transport
    

  

This helps reduce parent hesitation.

---

### **FAQ Section**

  

Short, clear answers to reduce uncertainty:

- “How soon will you reply?”
    
- “Can we visit the school?”
    
- “Do we need an instrument before starting?”
    
- “What happens after I send the message?”
    

  

Keep text minimal and reassuring.

---

### **CTA Section (Secondary)**

  

Secondary CTA for parents who want more information first:

  

**“Prefer more details first? Download the Lesson Info Pack.”**

  

Button links to the Info Pack form/section.
